Molten Cake

Ingredients:

  • Dark Chocolate: 120g
  • Unsalted Butter: 120g
  • Eggs: 2 large eggs
  • Granulated Sugar: 100g
  • All-Purpose Flour: 40g
  • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: A pinch
  • Optional: Cocoa Powder: 20g
  • Optional: Espresso Powder: 5g
  • Powdered Sugar (for dusting): For garnish

Click to prepare

Recipe:

  1. Preheat the Oven:

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

    Grease four individual ramekins (about 6 ounces each) with butter and lightly dust them with cocoa powder.

  2. Melt Chocolate and Butter:

    In a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water (double boiler method) or in the microwave, melt the dark chocolate and unsalted butter together until smooth.

    Stir occasionally to ensure even melting. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

  3. Prepare Batter:

    In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract until well combined.

    Slowly pour the melted chocolate mixture into the egg mixture while whisking continuously until smooth.

    Add a pinch of salt and mix well. If using, sift in cocoa powder and espresso powder and fold until fully incorporated.

    Finally, gently fold in the all-purpose flour until no streaks remain.

  4. Fill Ramekins:

    Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins, filling them about three-quarters full.

  5. Bake:

    Place the filled ramekins on a baking sheet and transfer them to the preheated oven.

    Bake for about 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still soft and jiggly.

  6. Serve:

    Carefully remove the molten chocolate cakes from the oven and let them cool for a minute.

    Then, using oven mitts or a kitchen towel, invert each ramekin onto a serving plate.

    Dust with powdered sugar and serve immediately.

  7. Enjoy:

    Dive into the decadent molten chocolate cake, savoring the warm, gooey center with each spoonful.

    Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgent treat.
